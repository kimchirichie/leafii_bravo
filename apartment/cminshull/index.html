Quantum Hacking Research Student

Institute of Quantum Computing. Waterloo, ON

During my eight month term at the IQC, I was given the opportunity to take on my own research project, a task normally left for Ph.D students, while still in my undergrad. Over the eight months, I took an idea from my supervising professor to concept, then prototype, and finally a full implementation with conclusive publishable data. We sought to uncover a previously unknown vulnerability in a commercial quantum cryptography platform and were successful. Tasks ranged from experimental planning, purchase and assembly of components and lasers, all the way to test automation and data analysis of results. This led me to the first author writing of a research paper which will soon be seeking publication.
